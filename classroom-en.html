Positive Classroom Management

This page is designed to help you build a safe and stimulating classroom environment that supports active learning and inquiry, rather than just keeping 'quiet'.

What is Classroom Management?

Classroom management is designing an organized, safe, and respectful learning environment. It includes building rules, routines, relationships, and managing time and space. It's proactive efforts that build a culture preventing most challenges from the start.

Principles of Positive Classroom Management

  • Building positive relationships with students (respect, active listening, knowing interests).
  • Clarity of expectations and rules from day one, involving students in creating them.
  • Focusing on positive reinforcement of desired behavior.
  • Responding calmly and firmly to inappropriate behavior away from emotional reactions.

Sample Classroom Management Plan

Rules

Clear and concise agreements for what is expected:

  • We respect others when they speak and listen to them.
  • We keep our bodies and property safe.
  • We do our best in learning.

Routines

Specific steps to organize the day:

  • Routine for entering the classroom and starting the lesson.
  • Procedures for asking for help or going to the restroom.
  • How to transition between activities or groups.

Classroom Scenarios and How to Respond

Scenario: A student constantly interrupts their peer during a discussion

Ineffective Response: Yelling at the student in front of everyone and kicking them out.

Positive Response: Visual reminder of the class agreement (e.g., pointing to the listening rule), and directing a direct question to the interrupting student after their peer finishes speaking.

Scenario: Chaos during the transition from individual work to group work

Ineffective Response: Getting angry and threatening to deduct points.

Positive Response: Calmly stopping everyone (using an agreed signal like a countdown), re-explaining the transition routine step-by-step, then practicing it again.

Classroom Management in the IB PYP Context

PYP classrooms rely on active learning and inquiry, which means constant movement, questioning, and experimentation. Classroom management here is not to silence students or limit their movement, but to organize this vitality and energy in the right direction.
