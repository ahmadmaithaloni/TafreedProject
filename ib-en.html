What is the International Baccalaureate (IB)?

This page is designed for educators to understand the IB philosophy, specifically the Primary Years Programme (PYP), and how to apply these principles within your 'Tafreed' professional journey.

Overview of IB

The IB is a global framework that places the holistic development of the learner at its core. While spanning multiple programs, Tafreed primarily focuses on the PYP to build a strong foundation for inquiry.

The Primary Years Programme (PYP)

  • Targeted at the 3-12 age group.
  • Learning is structured around inquiry units related to Transdisciplinary Themes.
  • The learner acts as a 'young researcher', rather than a passive recipient of information.

What is Inquiry-Based Learning?

Inquiry implies that learning begins with students' questions and natural curiosity. The teacher designs an environment enabling research, experimentation, and deep reflection.

Example: An activity where students formulate questions about climate change, experiment, and build models to find answers.

Roles of Teacher and Student

Teacher (Facilitator)

  • A learning facilitator, not the sole source of knowledge.
  • Designs thought-provoking inquiry units.
  • Guides and mentions instead of providing ready-made answers.

Student (Researcher)

  • An active and curious researcher.
  • Asks questions and co-selects learning approaches.
  • Experiments, learns from mistakes, and takes agency.

How does IB connect to the Tafreed project?

Tafreed empowers educators to design their professional development pathways just as they design student learning. IB's inquiry philosophy aligns with individualized training: every teacher has a unique starting point.
